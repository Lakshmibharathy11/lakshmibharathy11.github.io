About
Background

At RepuNEXT, I used Google Analytics and SEMrush to diagnose funnel drop-off and rebuild SEO targeting, driving a 25% increase in organic traffic. That was my first real exposure to using data to change business outcomes, not just describe them. Graduate school let me go deeper into the causal and statistical layer behind that kind of work.

My recent projects reflect that same throughline: a Bayesian Marketing Mix Model recommending a $4.8M budget reallocation across channels, and causal uplift modeling on over a million users showing that naive targeting wastes roughly 22% of ad spend. I've paired this with churn and CLV modeling, and more recently, hands-on work with modern AI tooling — LangGraph agents, RAG, and LLM evaluation pipelines — to understand how these systems behave in practice, not just in theory.

My thesis benchmarked five ML classifiers and two fine-tuned CNNs for eye-tracking-based gaze classification, reaching 0.94 cross-user accuracy, 12 points above the published baseline. Manuscript in preparation for publication.

Marketing Mix Modeling — Budget Allocator

Bayesian MMM using Google Meridian to quantify channel-level ROI across 5 channels on 156 weeks of geo-level data. VIF-informed priors handle collinearity where data alone can't isolate individual channel contributions; a constrained optimizer recommends budget reallocation, delivered through a Looker Studio dashboard.

$4.8M recommended reallocation · +0.5% projected conversion lift · mROI < 1.0 confirms saturation on lowest-performing channel
